The Married Puts Strategy

Explanation of what a Married Put is

Married Puts are not illegal. They're a perfectly fine trading strategy. They do offer baddies a loophole for resetting the timer on an FTD.

If you know the basics of Option Trading, then Married Puts are simple to understand. If not, you're fucked and you need to understand options to get this.

​

What's a Married Put?

A Married Put is when you buy a Call and a Put at the exact same strike and expiration date in the exact same moment in time. The PUT's strike must be IN THE MONEY e.g above the current stock price.

How do people create/open married puts?

  • People do this by making a Multi-Leg order which is an order that contains 2 trades to be executed at the same time.
  • They set the multi leg order to contain a "Please buy me a Call at $100 strike and a Put at $100 strike both expiring on the same date".
  • Don't forget, the
  • Once the order is submitted, both trades inside that single order are executed simultaneously thus creating a "Married Put".

It's a perfectly normal and legal way (although not popular) of hedging / buying insurance against near term volatility.

​

How do the baddies use them to kick the can?

Well, as i said, you get a call and a put at the exact same strike, both expiring on the same date.

To reset your FTD's and thus kick the can down road, you would need to:

  • Buy yourself some married puts. (Same strike/expiring Call AND Put)
  • Exercise the (I think ITM) call to get your 100 synthetic shares per call option contract.
  • Use those 100 shares to close your current FTDs.
  • By closing your current FTD's using your 100 synthetic shares you got from your call exercise, you're creating a new batch of 100 shares that will become FTD's eventually.

So basically you've closed the old FTD's and opened an equal amount of new ones. The new ones have their FTD timer reset, so the hedgie needs to do this all over again in some amount of time in order to kick the can again. Repeat ad' infinitum.
