# Title: The Securities and Exchange Commission today announced that 21 investment advisers and 6 broker-dealers have agreed to settle charges that they failed to timely file and deliver their client or customer relationship summaries – known as Form CRS – to their retail investors.
# Author: Dismal-Jellyfish
# Post URL: [https://www.reddit.com/r/Superstonk/comments/os7p0v/the_securities_and_exchange_commission_today/](https://www.reddit.com/r/Superstonk/comments/os7p0v/the_securities_and_exchange_commission_today/)


[Source](https://www.sec.gov/news/press-release/2021-139)

The SEC’s orders find that the investment advisers violated Section 204 of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 and Advisers Act Rules 204-1 and 204-5, and that the broker-dealers violated Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Exchange Act Rule 17a-14.  Without admitting or denying the findings, the firms agreed to be censured, to cease and desist from violating the charged provisions, and to pay the following civil penalties:

* [Altschuler, James Stephen](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5786.pdf), a Lexington, Massachusetts-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Canton Hathaway LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5788.pdf), a Providence, Rhode Island-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Carmel Capital Management LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5806.pdf), a Carmel, California-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Castle Wealth Planning LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5796.pdf), a Santa Barbara, California-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Cohen Klingenstein LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5798.pdf), a New York, New York-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $97,523 civil penalty.
* [Dynamic Trading Management LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5799.pdf), a White Plains, New York-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $10,000 civil penalty.
* [Eastside Financial Advisors LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5800.pdf), a Fayetteville, New York-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $10,000 civil penalty.
* [Embree Financial Group Inc.](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5791.pdf), a Chicago, Illinois-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $97,523 civil penalty.
* [Harold Davidson & Associates Inc.](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5797.pdf) a Los Angeles, California-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [John A. Bysko Associates](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5787.pdf), an Old Lyme, Connecticut-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Madden Funds Management Ltd.](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5792.pdf), an Oak Park, Illinois-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Medallion Wealth Advisors LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5789.pdf), a Farmington, Connecticut-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Mighty Oak Strong America Investment Company](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5802.pdf), a Mechanicsburg, Pennsylvania-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.

* [O’Brien Greene & Co. Inc.](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5803.pdf), a Media, Pennsylvania-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Paratus Financial Inc.](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5795.pdf), a Dallas, Texas-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $97,523 civil penalty.
* [Quantitative Asset Management LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5793.pdf), a Wayzata, Minnesota-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
* [Sauberan & Company LLC](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5801.pdf), a Lockport, New York-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $10,000 civil penalty.
* [Summit Financial Advisors Inc.](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2021/ia-5794.pdf), a Lebanon, Ohio-based investment adviser, has agreed to pay a $25,000 civil penalty.
