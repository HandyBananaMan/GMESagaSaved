Title: GME won't crash the market at 100k per share, not even close ...

Author: the_nebraskan

Post URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/GME/comments/ls35v0/gme_wont_crash_the_market_at_100k_per_share_not/

The current float (i.e. market tradeable shares) of GME is 45.05M shares source. Let's assume a theoretical maximum short interest of 100% (yes, I know it could be higher due to naked shorting) and let's also assume the shorts will have to cover at a median price of $100K, that would be a total of $4.5T (float x median price). To put that number into perspective, the market cap of just the S&P 500 is $31.3T source. As a percentage we are talking <15%. Furthermore, the S&P 500 is just one market. If we take the sum of the market caps for the three major US markets (DOW, NASDAQ, S&P) we have a total market cap of $8.2T source + $17.9T source + $31.3T = $57.4T. If we use this as our denominator, we now have a miniscule percentage of 7.8%. Call me crazy, but I don't think a dip in the total market cap of the US economy of 7.8% will cause a crash.

TLDR - A short squeeze of GME to $100k and beyond won't crash US markets based purely on capital requirements. Your tendies will be safe when we land on the moon. See you in Valhalla.
